Design of an aerator for fisheries applications

iHUB-AWaDH Internship Project

Abstract

In this internship project, we have tried to solve mainly two problems that are the uniformity of aeration and efficiency of aeration. In the present market which is dominated by surface aerators, the uniformity of aeration is very less as the aerator is placed in a fixed position. The efficiency of surface aerators is very low and due to this more power is consumed as they need to run for a longer time. In our solution, we have presented a way to uniformly distribute the aerated water across the pond with a bottom-up approach that will lead to faster and uniform aeration. Our aeration system is a two-stage aeration system. In the first stage, the unaerated water is pumped into a porous media-based U-tube type aeration system where the water is mixed with air using the venturi principle. The second aeration system is a tank with a sparger-based aeration system. The tank is designed such that it can hold the aerated water for a longer period of time so that the aeration of water and mass transfer takes place properly. The whole system is energy efficient and can be run on solar power. This aeration system is also incorporated with automation and IoT. The aeration starts and stops automatically based on the sensor feedback and can inform the condition of the pond to the farmers through SMS or web interface.
